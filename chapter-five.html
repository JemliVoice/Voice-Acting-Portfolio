Chapter Five

Chapter OneChapter TwoChapter ThreeChapter FourChapter SixChapter Seven

A blackout. Something I never thought I'd witness in this building. My first reaction was to think that I had imagined the blackout, but that thought left my mind quickly as soon as I realized I was not moving at all. I thought if at any point the eye-light-glare thing wanted to come back, now would be the time. I am stuck in the ILLIS, suspended over offices shrouded in darkness. I try the emergency button on the ILLIS, but nothing happens. I press it again, and again, and again. Just click, click, click. Completely dead.

"Lights!" I call out, "lights on." Definitely dead. If I had my proximity debug multi-tool, I could probably figure something out with it. Of course, I can't just bring something like that into work, even though I only use it as a hobbyist. Security here is tight and serious. One time, I tried to bring a little gadget in, nothing even close to the specs of my multi-tool, and they confiscated it before I could even enter the lobby. The company takes electricity, technology, and security very seriously, and therefore a blackout within the facility seemed impossible. Yet, here we are.

My mind launches into intrusive thoughts of all the ways I would perish--like the magnetic rails that held the ILLIS up lost power and would give out, sending me crashing down fourteen stories in pitch black, or the walls would move in on me and start squeezing and squeezing until my bones shattered and I'd become visceral, red mush, a splatter of used to be anatomy--

"OK! Enough of that." I scold myself out loud, my voice flattened out by the glass walls and swallowed by silence. I take deep breaths and try to calm myself before I hyperventilate and see things. "I...am...I am stuck, in an elevator!" I force myself to laugh. I laugh at how close the walls are to my body. The Feed always gave the illusion that the space inside the ILLIS was bigger somehow. It was always bright, colorful, and exciting for me. I laugh harder because when I'd stop, the silence would consume anything emitted from me. I worry that if I didn't make a noise, the silence would choose to gorge on me next. I laugh now, because the walls were just glass, inches away from me. I never noticed how my breath could fog up the glass. I laugh until I become lightheaded.

While still riding out the high of laughter, I tuck the folders under one arm and pull out my phone to call for help. My phone is just stuck on the homepage--it's on, but I can't do anything to it. Can't dial emergency, can't open any apps. I try my watch. Frozen as well. I'm trying the buttons on the watch, then again on the ILLIS.

What the hell? I wrap my arms around myself, holding the files tight and sink into the ground. I drop the folders next to me and sit there, in darkness and silence, running my hands through my short hair. I close my eyes and rest my forehead on my knees. How long am I going to be trapped here? I'm going to see something any second now, and I just don't want to deal with that right now. I can't even tell how long I had spent in here already, with my watch acting weird.

In all the ads and marketing regarding the ILLIS, I have not seen them specify the repairability of the machine. I know that would be due to security reasons, but it got me thinking. It is just a fancy elevator, after all. I thought of an old trick I was introduced to by someone in a hotel from when my mom and I were moving states. It was a really cheap hotel that was living in the past, never been upgraded and falling apart. The elevator there was a relic of time. It would be crazy to think that this trick would work, but I had nothing better to do.

I know one of the codes the company uses, thanks to Pterygium's endless demands. I get up and press the open and close button at the same time, and punch in the only code I know.

Absolutely nothing happened.

I growl, and slump back to the floor. I feel ridiculous for thinking that would work. My forehead finds my knees again. A loud beep shatters the silence, startling me. A few short bursts of electronic whirring followed, and the ILLIS starts vibrating, humming with electricity. Either I am saved, or this is going to be my premature death. The elevator lurches abruptly and a symphony of familiar buzzing and humming greets me, and then came the lights--dazzling, beautiful lights draping over the gray offices below me, and my Feed detonated onto the glass walls of the ILLIS, showing me that these bad-ass looking boots are on sale. Last comes my music, Siouxsie and the Banshees belts out "Send me forwards--say my feelings, But all the signals--send me reeling, Jigsaw Feeling" and I could cry with relief. The ILLIS picks itself up again as though there was never a blackout at all, and gracefully lands on one of the support and administrative floors.

"Trinity! Gobs, were you in the ILLIS when the blackout happened?!" A young woman with an intricately styled white wig at the front desk greets me in a worried tone.

"Oh yeah, it was freaky but I'm ok, Piper. Do you know what happened?" I ask her. I hate when people are worried about me, so I try to quickly move on.

"I have not received word from anyone yet, my guess is a freak blackout. It shouldn't happen again, though. We have safety measures within the facility to prevent this sort of thing, and I'm sure there's a team of people handling whatever is going on as we speak." She reassured me in a formal voice.

"I see...well, I have documents from Mr. Pterygium that he needed me to send through your department. Can I leave them with you?"

"Of course, I'll take care of that for you." Her glasses flashed, scanning the documents instantly and I watched her wrap the folders before sending them off through the folder chute, thinking of how I could phrase my question.

"So, Piper, what could have caused the blackout to begin with?" I ask nonchalantly. She touches her earpiece to accept an incoming transmission. She listens for a moment before turning back to face me fully.

"Sorry, just got a message. You asked me, what caused the blackouts? I'm sorry, but like I said I haven't received word on that. If you'll excuse me, there's something that requires my immediate attention. Chat ya later!" Piper stood, packing her tablet into a briefcase and giving me a polite smile before hurrying off to her task.

My FeedWatch buzzes, and I check the notification.

C. Pterygium: where are you.

I cringe, noticing just how long it took me to finish dropping off the documents he wanted me to handle. I might be able to get away with blaming it on the blackout. I talk into my FeedWatch to respond to my boss.

T. Lehman: My apologies; I was inside the Individual Laminar Lift System and upon the company-wide power outage, I was captive within it, without method of eluding the situation. Currently, it is my intent that I proceed into your office.

I read my response back to myself, impressed. I chose the talk-to-text that converts my words to make me sound like a snooty, intelligent person. It also makes most people I chat with take a long time to respond.

C. Pterygium: do so.

Not a bad response from Pterygium, but doesn't necessarily mean it's a good one, either. I pick up the pace and head for the escalator. It will take me longer to get to my boss' office, but I will not be taking an ILLIS until a technician themselves can tell me there will be no more blackouts today.

No one uses the escalator anymore; it is similar to the old elevators in that they both display general, popular Feeds that cater to most people. There were so many complaints about the default Feeds that as soon as the first row of ILLISes were integrated, most employees were late to various things across the building because everyone ditched the escalators in favor of trying the new personal elevator. Since the initial decline in productivity due to the ILLIS being all the rage, they removed most of the escalators to make room for more ILLISes. That smoothed things out for productivity, until today.

Upon entering the opening in the center of the building that leads to the escalator, I lay my eyes on the crowd that formed to surround it. All these people had the same thought as me. I join the crowd and we all shuffle our way towards a step. The glass dome bubbling the escalator displayed the boring ad-filled Feed and played songs advertising products, companies, and technologies. The ad I mindlessly watch is from Springz Platforms Inc., the company that owns Cheese and all the affiliates.

The logo, just a few lines and squiggles, shimmied to an animation before large, excited text pulsed out in front of everyone on the escalator. The intrusive nature made the people directly in front of the ad shift away from it as much as they could in the cramped space.

Here at Springz Inc., our reach is greater than ever! Here's a word from our CEO, live at our new headquarters on the atoll country of Tokelau!

The logo flashed again and faded into a gray building surrounded by crystal-like, turquoise ocean water. The windows reflected the vibrant blue, cloudless sky and lustrous water, which gave the appearance of a rectangular blue void within the gray skyscraper. The drone panned down to a woman with her long chestnut hair down and unbuttoned gray blazer over a white collared shirt. You can tell she's on island time.

My name is Harpüre Feaster, CEO of Springz Platforms Incorporated, here with PreserveX to let you know that our Advanced Technological Processing System, Safe-ari, is limitless! Springz has teamed up with PreserveX to identify and track endangered species in an ongoing effort to protect their habitat. PreserveX has used our surveillance drone footage alongside our ATPS Safe-ari around the new Springz headquarters to track and train intelligence systems on identifying animals in their habitats. It just goes to show what incredible feats companies can accomplish through our ATPS models around the world.

I remembered that a while ago, Springz was making plans to expand the platform's reach across the world. Learning about how technology improves the world brightens my day. Many countries like Tokelau have been taken by the rising ocean, but Springz managed to bring it back, which is simply incredible. The escalator neared the the floor of Mr. Pterygium's office. The last thing I saw on the Feed before I got off was Piper's dating ad. There's a new one from her around every month or two, when she changes her wig color. Piper, at least, can remain professional while using the dating ads on Cheese. She doesn't advertise in person or at work, whereas Reed, while putting up a dating ad on the Feed, still flirts and advertises his ad to any woman in proximity. For example, any time he updates even the most minute detail, he insists on showing me to ask what I think. Personally, I think it's pathetic, but I just tell him to log off.

The escalator rises to be level with the floor, and I step out. I take a deep breath and re-tuck my blouse into my trousers before going down the hall leading to the office belonging to my boss.
