Chapter Four

Chapter OneChapter TwoChapter ThreeChapter FiveChapter SixChapter Seven

Geoffe Lehman. Could the name on the document before me truly belong to my father? Living in one of the busiest cities in America, as well as having moved from one side of the country to the other; to this day I have not met anyone named Geoffe who spells their name exactly like my father, G-e-o-f-f-e. Let alone one with my last name.

Could this be a code name? How could it be that somebody decided to use that specific name as a code name for a classified subject to be contained at Cheese Inc. headquarters? What could that possibly mean?

I rifle through the papers for more information. I was tempted to scan them with my FeedWatch, but reminded myself that these are supposed to be classified files. Then it struck me. I am not being smart right now. I was most likely being monitored by the cameras. If a person walked in, I'd be covered by the tabletshelf. However, the cameras around here can easily walk along the walls and ceiling to get an angle of whatever they'd want.

I steady myself, trying to act like a casual secretary. I have to hurry and get out of here before I'm tracked, if I'm not already being monitored. Skimming the pages as I sort them has not lent me more meaningful understanding. It was more legally-worded phrases, for instance:"all personnel in possible perception of contact with entity Geoffe Lehman are to be assumed as collaborators." I gather the folders and calmly walk out of the library and back to the ILLIS to turn the documents into administration.

My mind is full, but I have to stay focused. I cannot under any circumstance afford an attack right now. Just the thought of having one makes my heart pound and tense my shoulders in anticipation. My Feed bummed me out last time, so I closed my eyes to recall something my mother taught me a long, long time ago...

Breathe deeply three times, each breath an intent. One breath for calm--

I inhale deep and slow, thinking of the word 'calm', and ocean waves lap in my eyes--

one for focus--

I exhale, and grip the file jackets tighter--

And one for--

"Lehman, working hard or hardly working?" A nasally voice barks out from behind me. I gasp, nearly shrieking, causing my colleague to cackle with laughter. "I'm so sorry, I forgot how easily you scare."

My lips tighten, trying to hold back anger. "Reed." I have nothing to say to him. My heart is still racing, and I try to convince it that I am not in danger. No, Reed isn't dangerous; just a really annoying and desperate colleague who happens to be my age and thinks that fact is an instant pass given to him to flirt with me.

"So, you lost or something?" He winks, clearly trying to call back to how we met. I roll my eyes; he brings that up every other time he catches me off guard.

It was my first week at Cheese Inc., and the tour (if you could call it that) of the nineteen-story building had me more confused about the layout than before I was even given a tour. The supervisor, Rheaya, didn't bother showing all the floors, and her reason was "if you don't know what that room or level is for, then it clearly doesn't concern you". Some levels even require a special badge, so it's not as though I can just go to those whenever I feel like it. Those clearly aren't my problem, but what is my problem is how Pterygium has me going around talking to managers on floors of the massive building I was never introduced to.

On one of those runs, I finally got to use the ILLIS and instantly was so overwhelmed with my Feed burgeoning before me, ads pleading for my attention, and music sonorously attempting to rattle my brains--I couldn't even think of what floor to be on. I ended up on the marketing and branding floor, where Reed works. The walls, doors, floor, everything within the ILLIS is glass; allowing anyone to see inside, with the only privacy coming from whatever is on your Feed... meaning everyone got to witness how I was frantically trying to press the right buttons. Especially the person who was waiting for the ILLIS: Reed.

Of course, at the time, he didn't even look human to me. More like a faceless, hunched over homunculus. I knew the stretched and drooping mouth coming out of a pile of flesh belonged to a human face, just what the human I was meeting for the first time looked like was distorted by my experience of the ILLIS ride.

I desperately tried to steel myself but unfortunately, the homunculus decided it was a good time to introduce itself.

"I've not seen a face like yours around here before. You new? I'm guessing you must be from like, a farm or something. Never been in an ILLIS before? It's no biggie. I know it's a personal elevator, but if you'd like, I could ride with you, to you know, show you how it works. Of course, only platonically though. Hey, I'm Reed, by the way. Been here since the first ILLISes were installed, so I'm pretty good with them. So, you gonna tell me your name or do I have to pry it out of you?" It chortled at the question.

The more it talked, the more I thought the homunculus image was very fitting. I wasn't listening to half of what was being said, but my overwhelming feelings from the ILLIS were fading, being replaced by feelings of bafflement and amusement at how forward this person was. His head started shrinking back into one with more human features; his cheekbones growing outward from the skin and pulling the jaw inward. The hunch wasn't my imagination, but it wasn't as dramatic as I had exaggerated it.

"Yeah, I'm new. That was my first time in an ILLIS, but I'm not from a farm. I just didn't expect to be violated and bombarded with ads and shit from my feed. What a pleasure it is to meet you." That last part came out very sarcastic, because it was. I don't think Reed understood, he seemed flattered even; standing up straighter and fixing the collar on his shirt.

"Ah, well, of course my offer still stands. Soo...name?" He stuck out his hand, and reluctantly I shook it. If he's been here for as long as he said, then he probably knows the building well enough to send me to where I needed to go. I decided to play along.

"Trinity. I'm trying to get to human resources, am I even somewhat close?"

"Trinity," he drew out the last syllable, as if tasting the word coming out of his mouth. I held back a gag. "Just like from The Matrix. Happens to be my favorite movie. As far as the floor you need, you couldn't be more off-base."

"No, not like from The Matrix. Anyway, just let me know which floor HR is, and I'll be out of your hair. I have to file a report someone made on my boss Charles, and I've also got loads more work to get done for him, so I really have to get a move on."

"Charles...oh, you mean Mr. Pterygium? He hates being called Charles. Ask me how I know." He crossed his arms and gave me side-eye that signified he really wanted me to ask. I hadn't realized someone could be this dense, so I continued to stare at him, waiting for him to carry on. He didn't seem to notice any of my body language, and pressed his own narrative. "Wait, so you're the new secretary for him? Oh, then I'd seriously better help you out here. He needs you to go to floor thirteen, you'll have to make a right down the corridor straight out of the ILLIS, and then you'll see a sign that leads you to Sheighlly, she's in charge of employee reports."

"Thanks so much! Better get going. Just as much as Charles hates being called Charles, he also hates me being late." I rushed back to the ILLIS before Reed could ask to squeeze in and show me around.

Reed seems to have a worst-timing watch, because he shows up right on time, rain or shine. The only time I didn't mind his company was that first interaction with him, and it was only because he was useful. Thinking on it, he might be useful again for me now.

"Nope, not lost. Question for you: ever meet a Geoffe, spelled with a G?" I'm not sure what I hope for his response to be.

"Jeff with a G?..." He crosses his arms and rubs his chin. "You mean, would it be, J-E-G-H? Wait, or like, G-E-F-F?" He tilts his head while pressing his lips together, furrowing his eyebrows in a thoughtful manner. I let out a deep breath and immediately rub my temple.

"Yeah. I can tell you haven't." I can't tell if he heard me because I notice him mumbling something.

"J, no.. G-E-P-H..."

"Well, Reed, thanks for all your help." I start heading for the ILLIS again.

"Wait, Trin, you're just gonna ask me that and not tell me why?" Reed pouts to my back. "I don't even know how I helped you just now."

"Oh, don't worry about it," I turn back to him with a corporate smile. "You've helped me more than you can know." That much is true. It confirms something I have no idea how to begin to process. Reed furrows his brows, this time with confusion. I sigh. It's not his fault he can't spell an obscure archaic name. It wasn't a true confirmation.

"You've lived here your whole life, right? Never met a Geoffe, spelled G-E-O-F-F-E? Or even seen the name written in a newsfeed?" I cross my arms, hugging the file jackets close.

"Ah, see, I never consider the French. Can't say I've seen a name like that or knew anyone with it, nope!" He shrugs dramatically. I nod. This means I have to see what's in containment. My curiosity is eating me alive.

"So, how well do you know the building, then?" First, I have to see if he's going to be a rat or not.

"Ha! So you are lost again! I think I have a sense for when you get lost in the building. Luckily for you, I know it pretty well. Of course, I have it mapped out within my FeedWatch, I'm surprised you haven't done that yet. Where do you need to go?" I'm pretty tech savvy, yet Reed makes me feel like I'm in the Nipkow disk days. Since when could you map out an entire building with your FeedWatch?

"Oh, Pterygium is having a meeting right now and he needs me to go to containment to...deliver these files." I hate lying and I'm not very good at it. I cross my fingers for him to buy it.

"Hmm, containment. Now there's a floor I haven't been to. Easy enough to look it up on the Watch." He pulled it up and started up the map. "You got the clearance badge from Mr. Pterygium to get in?" Another thing I haven't thought of.

"Eh.." I feel my heartbeat accelerate. I don't have an answer.

"Let me guess, you forgot it in his office?" I silently thank any higher power for Reed.

"Yes! I did. And I can't go back in there, because he's in the middle of a meeting and if I interrupt, he will...probably twist my head off." I nod again.

"Jeez! Better not bother him. Hey, is that why you were asking for Geoffe? Maybe he has a clearance badge. Only thing is, the Jeff I know spells his name the regular way. And he works in the financial department. He wouldn't have containment-level clearance."

"Well, aren't we in a conundrum. Any way we can get there without the badge?" Reed barks out a laugh at my question.

"No, of course not."

"Ah, well it's alright, I've got another task to fulfill for Pterygium before he's finished with the meeting, so I'd better hurry and do that." I have to deliver the papers to where Pterygium originally asked me to. "Thanks again, Reed."

"Yeah, no problem! I'll see you later, when you're lost again." He winks at me before we split ways. There will be time to look into the mystery, but right now I should do my job before I lose it. I head to the ILLIS, rushing my deep breaths and trying to look forward to the ride. As the ILLIS comes down, I check my phone to make sure nothing would jump-scare me. I had a nice song queued up, and one of my favorite graphic designer's new gallery on my Feed.

Once the ILLIS lands, I step in and my Feed connects. Advertisements presenting Springz Advanced Technological Processing System's new design programs and the art from the designer that I follow paints the glass walls before me. I take my time to admire the art. Something bright and flickering reflects in my glasses straight into my eye, and while the glare is annoying, I try to ignore it and shift in the small space of the elevator. My eyes scan a new art piece that slides in front of me, but the glare comes back into view. Fed up, I take off my glasses and my stomach sinks. The light that was reflecting from my glasses still hovered in front of me, in the same shape as the lens.

Not again...

The light fluctuates, stretching and sliding into a new shape. Even without my glasses on, I can tell the light metamorphoses itself into an eye. The eye-light is the same shape as the one formed from words on the document. It blinks, and the lights in the entire building cut out, including the ones in the ILLIS. The entire elevator stops, trapping me in the dark, confined space in the air.
