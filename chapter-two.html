Chapter Two

Chapter OneChapter ThreeChapter FourChapter FiveChapter SixChapter Seven

"Here's your latte, sir, I had it made just the way you like it with coconut milk and
whipped cream topped with cinnamon." I hold out the steaming cup to my boss.

"Coconut milk?" Mr. Pterygium sighs, his eyes seeing through the carpet. He let the silence hang in the room for a moment, then stands from his chair and reaches over his desk in order to snatch the drink from my hands. I wince as a scalding splash of coffee hits my arm, instantly staining my pale yellow blouse. "Did I not ask for soy milk with coconut flakes? I don't like coconut milk anymore, but this will do."

I don't dare argue with him; I learn from my mistakes. Mr. Pterygium always pulls stunts like this-- he makes me get a very specific item for him for a long period of time, decides he doesn't like it anymore, then expects me to read his mind.

It's almost comical. Almost.

He takes a long, loud sip. When he put the cup down, he unknowingly left a line of whipped cream on his upper lip. He motions to the end of his laminate, geometric desk--specifically to the two file jackets and binder full of documents. "Right, I've got these folders sitting here on my desk, you see? They're real papers, not digital, and I need them stamped by the legal division. I've got a meeting in a few minutes, so the faster you get this done the better." He slurps his drink again, swiveling his chair away from me to face his computer monitor.

As if I received a notification in my mind, the employee's phone call on the ILLIS pops into my head. "Mr. Pterygium, I have a question--"

"What? No no no, it's simple, really. Take these documents, the folders on my desk, and take them to the administrative floor. The legal side. Those guys will take it from there. Get to it." He waves me off, not bothering to look at me when he spoke. Sips the latte, leaving more whipped cream on his lip. I bristle inside at his condescension.

"No, I was wondering about what one of your workers said. He was talking to someone about containment going offline?" My boss begins turning around very slowly, still not meeting my gaze. "Has there been a data leak or something I should be aware of?"

Mr. Pterygium's eyes flash and he finally glares at me. "That's of no concern to you!" Whipped cream flies from his mouth and lands on my blouse. I'm starting a collection of spilled beverages today. "Your job is simple. I have rules you need to follow: do not ask questions, and do what you are asked to do from me." He counted off on his fingers in an exaggerated manner. "You agreed to that when you got hired! And, I hired you as my assistant, so act like one. GET OUTA MY OFFICE!" With one hand he picks up all of the folders and throws them straight at me.

I caught maybe one folder and fall on my knees to collect what was flung from the stack. Without another word I run from his office, one of the file jackets threatening to slip from my arms. A vulture's shadow soars in front of me, but I don't dare look up. My vision blurs and my face burns and my throat feels knotted and it's hard to breathe but I run. I run until I slam into a wall, and then continue running along the wall until a door handle snatches the strap of my bag and I nearly fly face-first into the floor, but I drop the files to catch myself which causes a paper explosion. I lie on the floor for a moment, catching my breath, looking at the papers around me.

The angry image of Mr. Pterygium throwing papers at me, spitting on me, yelling at me, burns in my mind. How dare I have thoughts and feelings. Every day he expects me to show up and shut up, bow and break my back to serve his demands. My heart boils with hurt and indignation.

I realize I am still laying there on the floor, watching the black vulture circle above my head, and acting very unprofessional. At least only the cameras witnessed my fall...I just hope nobody watches the footage. I push myself up to start gathering the documents and reposition my bag back to my shoulder. My hands sting from breaking my fall and all the paper cuts I collected.

From what I briefly glanced at, the documents are a record or a log of something. I groan at the thought of sorting these back into their correct folders. I clench my jaw. If my boss wasn't such a prick, these papers would have been organized already and I could have done the task by now. At least nothing fell out of the binder.

I scoop the files in a way as to not disturb the order in which they fell, hoping that would make the job of sorting them into the right folders easier. I push myself to my feet and regard the door that nearly killed me. I ran blurry-visioned into another executive office--Bob P. Newman it said on the door--but luckily the owner of this one was not inside. The quicker I get away from the scene the better. The commons library would be a quiet place for me to sort the files without eyes on me. Nobody goes there at this time of day.

I make my way to the ILLIS, scan my badge, and feel a small bit of relief at the thought of seeing my Feed before I do the task of filing the documents. The ILLIS lands, and Mr. Newman steps out. I feel crazy lucky that he just missed the spectacle.

"Morning, Mr. Newman," I smile lightly. He is aloof, staring straight ahead down the hall that leads to his office. He pounds past me and I enter the ILLIS. Mr. Newman never acknowledges my existence, but this time he was in much more of a rush than I usually ever see him. He's the type of senior director that sits on ass all day. Pterygium did say he had a meeting, and maybe old Bob was just late for it after grabbing a doughnut for the morning. I don't think too much about it as I watch my friend's dog do a trick on the glass walls. Yeats' Grave by The Cranberries plays on the speakers. My mom also appeared. I feel a sudden pang, thinking about her, my dad--and the anxiety worsens as the music crescendos in my head.

"In a grave" Dolores's voice rings out, "in a grave" I notice I'm breathing hard, and on cue fleshy and vein-like growth crept into the ILLIS. I close my eyes just as the growth started bleeding. I don't know what to do--this happened as I was looking at my Feed. I've never encountered a situation in which the Feed didn't help me calm down. I clench my eyes tight, burying myself behind the folders in my arms. My glasses jam against my nose but I continue to press my face into the folders. I take a deep breath and focus hard. I think of the dog doing tricks.

The music continues, not knowing or caring what I am going through. I recognize, though, it isn't pounding in my head like it did a second ago. As my heart slows and stops trying to beat out of my chest, I open my eyes. The wet, muscly flesh clings to the glass walls, pulsing rhythmically to the bass of the song and shedding brown blood as it did so. The elevator also began slowing, getting ready to level itself with the floor. I sigh, and as soon as the doors open I rush out, careful to mind the flesh growth and its blood on the floor.

Happy to put that behind me, I make my way to the library and claim a table in the back, with a tabletshelf behind me. I toss my bag onto the table and spread out the documents before me, read the names on the folders, then sort them on the table in a way that makes sense for me. The binder thankfully kept in shape and for the most part, the documents from the file jackets did stay somewhat together and I can tell which folder they came from. There are a lot of strays, however. Completely out of order, gathered in a rush. I know I'd have to read them to know where they went. I don't like that idea because I know I am going to mess something up. Maybe I should just give them to the legal team as is...but the thought of messing up this badly and having someone else clean it up for me... I can't.

I'm a secretary, after all, I should be able to sort files. I steady myself. I might even learn something new. I read the folders again: Containment Procedures for LMN1, and the other just had LMN1 written on it. My eyes skim the stray papers. Legal terms pepper the documents, making my mind feel like mush. Ok, one at a time. I pick up one from the top.

"Writ of certiorari...uh...you go to this one," I mumble, moving the page to LMN1. I'm in way over my head. I remind myself, just one at a time. I pick up the next page. Some sort of log. A Memorandum For All Departments Of Containment. I scan the page, and my attention is seized by a particular sentence.

"No information from entity Geoffe Lehman is to be recognized as a statement of reality and any speculative information presented by entity Geoffe Lehman is to be recorded proxy via the authorized communication personnel."

I read the sentence over and over again. This just can't be...

Lehman...of course, LMN1...

I couldn't believe what I was reading. I really couldn't tell if this was my condition playing tricks on me, or if I had read that man's name just now. As I stare dumbfounded, it took me a second to realize that the words slowly begin to ascend and grow from out of the page. Rising, it morphs; black ink swirls in the air, some of it solidifies into a dot, the rest of the ink surrounds it as striations, and as the ink settled in place I realize the words have turned themselves into an iris. The inky black pupil bores its gaze into me, as if it wants me to understand something. It stares, and I can't do anything but stare back. Through my fear, I feel like this eye would not cause harm. But I don't understand what it wants from me. The iris, a perfect sphere, holds my gaze for a moment longer, and almost as if disappointed, slinks back onto the page.

The document lies before me, just as it was before the text grew out of it. I snap out of my trance and grab the page again. Sure enough, Geoffe Lehman was written as the subject. I normally see crazy things throughout the day, but today was especially excessive. I am exhausted, and I've barely started my shift. I normally can handle seeing things, because I know they aren't actually there. It's just my messed up mental state. This document, despite that, is real and tangible. I read the name over and over, the letters starting to blend together in my eyes, but I knew it was real. Geoffe Lehman.
